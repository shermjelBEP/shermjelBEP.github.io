What We Do

Month after month.

We work alongside your team.
Your teachers, coaches, principals, and curriculum leaders are the ones doing the work. Our job is to be in it with them. We sit in PLCs, plan alongside grade-level teams, and build decisions together with the staff who know your students best. Month after month, as a member of the team.

Coaching Engagements

Four ways we can
work with your school.

Every engagement is scoped to where you are. Start with a one-month Audit, move through Design and Implementation, or build a custom partnership around a specific priority. Each stands on its own, and each includes EdScale for your team.

1 Phase One
Audit
1 Month

Before we build anything, we find out what's really going on.

Our Work
Data & Assessment ReviewAnalyze ILEARN, Checkpoints, and internal data to identify priority gaps and trends.
Curriculum & Planning AlignmentAudit current pacing and materials against IDOE item specs and standards rigor.
Instructional Practice InsightObserve math instruction to assess alignment, engagement, and cognitive demand.
Systems & Staff Support ReviewEvaluate how current roles, data practices, and PD structures support math outcomes.
What You Walk Away With
Math Performance ProfileStrengths, gaps, and trends across grades tied to ILEARN and Checkpoint data.
Aligned Curriculum SnapshotHow your current materials and plans align (or don't) with IDOE item specs.
Instructional Practice SummaryClassroom observation takeaways and high-leverage areas for growth.
Strategic Action MapA practical plan outlining next steps for instructional focus and team support.
2 Phase Two
Design
3 Months

We build a plan your team uses every week.

Our Work
Collaborative Focus Area SelectionMeet with leadership to pick 1–2 math priorities based on your school's data.
Pacing & Assessment PlanningBuild or adjust a math pacing calendar aligned to Checkpoints, item specs, and ILEARN.
Team Support ToolkitEquip leaders and coaches with planning tools, observation look-fors, and PD decks.
Mini Learning CycleFacilitate a 3–4 week try-it phase on one focus area with a light feedback loop.
What You Walk Away With
Targeted Math Focus PlanA school-selected priority with clear next steps and success indicators.
Aligned Pacing CalendarA practical pacing map incorporating Checkpoints, priority standards, and ILEARN alignment.
PD + Coaching ToolkitReady-to-use planning guides, look-for tools, and slides for internal PD.
Math Strategy Integration MapA one-page view of how meetings, roles, and routines support your math strategy.
3 Phase Three
Implement
10 Months

We stay through the hard middle. The part where plans usually collapse.

Our Work
Monthly Coaching & Progress MonitoringRegular check-ins with school leaders to adjust strategies based on real-time data.
Checkpoint + ILEARN StrategyA clear assessment roadmap with routines for Checkpoints and ILEARN prep.
Learning Cycle ImplementationLead 2–3 focused sprints with turnkey PD tools and classroom-ready resources.
Data Systems + Mid-Year RecalibrationBuild data systems and conduct a mid-year reset with a year-end reflection.
What You Walk Away With
Full-Year ILEARN + Checkpoint StrategyA pacing-aligned assessment plan that supports standards mastery and state readiness.
Custom Data Collection SystemTools and guidance for gathering and using data in leadership, coaching, and PLCs.
Embedded Learning Cycles2–3 focused implementation sprints with ready-made materials and PD support.
Year-End Strategy SummaryA closing report and next-year roadmap to sustain and build on the work.

Our Approach

We sit in your building.
Month after month.

We embed with your leadership team across a full engagement. We show up in classrooms, data meetings, and PLCs on a monthly cadence. Every partnership moves at the pace of your staff and the reality of your calendar.

That's harder to scale. It's also what moves student outcomes. Every partnership is built around one question: is this plan best for the kids in this building, and can the adults sustain it?

"A real strategy survives past October. It survives a bad week, a staffing change, and the week before spring break."
